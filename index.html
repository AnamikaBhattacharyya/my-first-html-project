Perfect Margherita Pizza Recipe

Ingredients (for one 12-inch pizza)

For the Dough (makes 2 pizzas):

  • 3 ½ cups (440g) all-purpose flour or '00' flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 packet (2 ¼ tsp) active dry yeast
  • 1 ¼ cups (300ml) warm water (110°F or 45°C)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil

For the Toppings:

  • ½ cup (120ml) San Marzano tomato puree or crushed peeled tomatoes
  • Salt to taste
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 1-2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 6-8 oz (170-225g) fresh mozzarella (preferably mozzarella di bufala), sliced or torn
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Optional: grated Parmigiano-Reggiano for finishing

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Make the Dough (at least 8 hours in advance)

  1. In a bowl, dissolve yeast and sugar in warm water. Let sit for 5-10 minutes until foamy.
  2. In a large bowl, mix flour and salt. Create a well in the center and pour in the yeast mixture and olive oil.
  3. Mix with a spoon or your hands until a shaggy dough forms.
  4. Turn onto a floured surface and knead for about 8-10 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  5. Place in an oiled bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let rise at room temperature for 1 hour.
  6. Punch down and divide into two balls. Place in covered containers and refrigerate for 6-24 hours. Let sit at room temp for 1 hour before using.

Step 2: Prepare Sauce

  1. Use good-quality canned San Marzano tomatoes. Crush them by hand or puree briefly in a blender.
  2. Add a pinch of salt and a drizzle of olive oil. Optionally, add a touch of oregano, but traditional Margherita pizza keeps it very simple.

Step 3: Preheat Your Oven

  1. Preheat your oven to the highest temperature possible (500-550°F / 260-290°C) for at least 45 minutes.
  2. Place a pizza stone or steel on the middle or lower rack during preheat.

Step 4: Assemble the Pizza

  1. On a floured surface, stretch the dough into a 12-inch round. Avoid using a rolling pin to preserve air pockets.
  2. Transfer to a floured pizza peel or parchment paper.
  3. Spread a thin layer of tomato sauce over the dough, leaving a border.
  4. Add pieces of mozzarella evenly.
  5. Tear and scatter fresh basil leaves (some prefer to add basil after baking to avoid browning).
  6. Drizzle lightly with olive oil.

Step 5: Bake

  1. Slide the pizza onto the hot stone/steel.
  2. Bake for 7-10 minutes, or until the crust is golden and blistered, and cheese is bubbling and lightly browned.

Step 6: Finish and Serve

  1. Remove from oven, rest for a minute.
  2. Optionally, add more fresh basil and a sprinkle of Parmigiano-Reggiano.
  3. Slice and serve hot!
